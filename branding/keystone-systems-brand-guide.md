# Keystone Systems — Brand Guide

## Positioning

Keystone Systems is a software engineering consultancy providing architecture, technical strategy, and senior-level engineering judgment for growing companies. The founder is a former Principal/Staff-level engineer (Stripe, Microsoft). The brand should read as established, credible, and technical — a systems-advisory firm, not a narrow "we write code" shop. It supports a range of engagement types: net-new development, hardening AI-generated/prototype code for production, business process automation, technical due diligence for acquisitions, and AI training & setup.

Tone reference points: Stripe, Vercel, Linear — precise, restrained, no startup-mascot energy, no gradients or glow effects.

## Logo Concept

The mark is an abstract wedge — a rounded trapezoid, wide at the top and tapering toward the base. It references the literal geometry of a keystone (the load-bearing wedge at the top of an arch) without any literal stone, castle, or arch imagery. The shape reads as structural, precise, and load-bearing, in keeping with the "architectural/engineering drawing precision" direction from the original brief — abstract and modern rather than decorative.

The wordmark pairs `KEYSTONE` (bold) with `SYSTEMS` (lighter weight, wide tracking) beneath or beside it, set in a clean geometric sans-serif in the spirit of Inter / Söhne / IBM Plex Sans.

## Color Palette — "Blueprint"

Selected for its direct tie to the brand's engineering-drawing framing (precision, technical drafting) rather than a generic corporate navy.

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Blueprint Navy | `#14324D` | Icon, wordmark, primary brand color |
| Accent | Technical Blue | `#3E7CB1` | Secondary wordmark line ("SYSTEMS"), links, highlights — used sparingly |
| Neutral | Slate | `#8C97A6` | Body text on light backgrounds, secondary UI elements |
| Background (light) | Off-white | `#F5F7FA` | Default page/background |
| On dark | White | `#FFFFFF` | Logo and text on navy or dark backgrounds |
| On dark, secondary | Pale Blue | `#B7CBDD` | "SYSTEMS" line when logo is reversed on dark |

Two other navy directions were considered and set aside: **Ink Navy** (`#0B1D33`, near-black, safest/most Stripe-like) and **Executive Slate** (`#1B2A41`, more muted/advisory-toned). Either remains a reasonable fallback if Blueprint reads too saturated in a given application.

## Typography

Clean geometric sans-serif — Inter, Söhne, or IBM Plex Sans (or system equivalents: Helvetica Neue, Arial). `KEYSTONE` set bold with tight tracking; `SYSTEMS` set lighter/medium weight with wide letter-spacing as a secondary line.

## Usage Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use the navy or accent-blue versions on light backgrounds, white version on dark or navy backgrounds.
- Use the pure-black version for single-color print, stamps, or contexts where color can't be guaranteed.
- Maintain clear space around the mark roughly equal to the height of the wedge icon.
- Keep the wedge's proportions intact when scaling (don't stretch).

**Don't:**
- Don't add gradients, drop shadows, or glow effects.
- Don't recolor the mark outside the defined palette.
- Don't place the navy or black version on a dark background (use the white version instead).
- Don't add literal stone, brick, or arch texture to the wedge — it stays flat and abstract.
