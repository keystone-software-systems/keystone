# Keystone Systems — Landing Page Prompt

## Company Context

**Keystone Systems** is a software engineering consultancy specializing in architecture, technical strategy, and senior-level engineering judgment for growing companies. The founder is a former Principal/Staff-level software engineer with a background at Stripe and Microsoft. The brand should feel established, credible, and technical — not playful or startup-trendy. Positioning leans toward technical judgment and systems-advisory work, not a narrow "we write code" shop.

**Voice/tone guidelines:**
- Plain, direct, low-pressure — no hype, no credential name-dropping, no em dashes
- Confident through clarity, not through superlatives ("senior engineering judgment," not "world-class rockstar developers")
- Speaks to a technical or semi-technical buyer (CTO, VP Eng, founder) who values substance over flash

---

## Site Structure

**Primary nav:** Home · Solutions (dropdown) · About · Contact

### Solutions dropdown — one landing page per deliverable

1. **Net New Development** — greenfield software builds from scratch
2. **Vibe-Code to Production** — hardening AI-generated/prototype code into production-ready, maintainable systems
3. **Business Process Automation** — automating manual/operational workflows
4. **Acquisition Due Diligence** — technical inspection and risk assessment for companies evaluating a software-driven acquisition
5. **AI Training & Setup** — helping teams stand up and adopt AI tooling/workflows
6. **Existing Codebase Improvement** — dropping into an existing codebase for audits, refactors, feature development, and ongoing improvement (not just greenfield work)

Each Solutions page should follow a consistent template so the section feels cohesive:
- Headline naming the specific problem this service solves
- 2–3 sentence description of what the engagement looks like in practice
- "Who this is for" — the type of company/situation that fits this service
- "What you get" — a short list of concrete deliverables or outcomes
- A single clear call-to-action (e.g., "Talk through your situation")

## Homepage

**Hero section:**
- Headline: value-prop-forward, not name-forward (avoid explaining what "Keystone" means — keep that private)
- Candidate directions for headline copy: "Senior engineering judgment, without the full-time hire" or "The technical decisions that are hard to undo — made right the first time"
- Subheadline: one sentence naming the breadth of engagement types (new builds, hardening prototypes, audits, automation, due diligence) without listing all six exhaustively
- Primary CTA: leads to Contact or a scoping call booking
- Secondary CTA: "See what we do" — scrolls to or links into the Solutions overview

**Below the fold:**
- Brief "How we work" section — 3-step engagement model (e.g., Scope → Build/Advise → Handoff)
- Compact grid or list previewing all six Solutions, each linking to its own page
- Founder credibility section — background (Stripe, Microsoft, current staff/senior engineering role) stated plainly, no self-congratulatory language
- Closing CTA section before the footer

## Design Direction

- Clean, minimal, technical — closer to Stripe, Linear, or Vercel in visual language than a typical "agency" site
- Monochrome-first palette with a single accent color; avoid gradients, glow effects, or trendy "AI-generated" visual clichés
- Typography: clean sans-serif (Inter, Söhne, or IBM Plex Sans in spirit)
- Generous whitespace; avoid dense marketing-page clutter or stock photography
- No cutesy illustrations, mascots, or overly rounded UI elements
- Should work equally well as a single-scroll page (mobile) and a full desktop layout with the Solutions dropdown in the nav

## What to Avoid

- Nothing that reads as religious or denominational — the name's personal meaning is private
- No generic tech clichés (circuit boards, binary code, abstract glowing orbs)
- No overselling language ("world-class," "10x," "cutting-edge") — the brand voice is understated confidence
- Don't make the six Solutions pages feel like disconnected microsites — shared template and visual language across all of them
